Cheat Sheet for FDI in Single Brand Retail Trading

India’s Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) policy in Single Brand Retail Trading (SBRT) has undergone significant changes, making it easier for global brands to enter the market while ensuring local economic benefits.

FDI Limits & Approval Process

100% FDI is permitted in SBRT under the automatic route (since Jan 2018), eliminating the need for government approval. Earlier, approval was required for FDI beyond 49%.

Local Sourcing Requirement (30% Mandate)

If FDI exceeds 51%, at least 30% of the goods’ value must be sourced from India, with some portion from MSMEs, village and cottage industries, artisans, and craftsmen.

For the first 5 years, global sourcing from India can count toward this requirement; afterward, it applies solely to Indian operations.

E-Commerce & Physical Store Requirement

Retailers can sell online but must set up a physical store within two years of starting online sales. Products must be sold under a single brand, globally registered.

Impact of Liberalization

  • Boosts consumer choices
  • Encourages local manufacturing
  • Creates jobs
  • Strengthens supply chains

Challenges

  • Balancing local sourcing with quality
  • Regulatory navigation
  • Competition from domestic & e-commerce players

Final Thoughts: India’s SBRT FDI policy offers huge potential, but requires careful compliance and sourcing strategy for success.

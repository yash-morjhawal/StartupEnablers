Raising funds from friends and family is a common early-stage startup strategy. While informal, it must comply with legal frameworks under Indian corporate law.

Valuation Reports

  • Required under Companies Act, 2013 for private placement.
  • For non-residents, FEMA compliance requires SEBI-registered Merchant Banker or CA valuation.

Secretarial Compliance

  • Form SH-7: For increasing authorized share capital.
  • MGT-14: Filed with RoC when private placement is approved.
  • PAS-4: Offer Letter for private placement.
  • PAS-3: Filed after allotment of shares; funds cannot be used before filing.

Investment Agreements

Even with friends/family, have a formal agreement covering:

  • Nature of investment
  • Equity distribution
  • Voting rights
  • Exit mechanisms
  • Dispute resolution

Conclusion: Friends and family funding can be valuable, but must be legally compliant to protect both founders and investors.
